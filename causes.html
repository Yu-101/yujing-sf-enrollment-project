Main Causes of Ocean Pollution

Plastic waste makes up most of the debris found in the ocean. Items like bags, bottles, and packaging break into microplastics that endanger marine life.

Every year, millions of tons of plastic enter the ocean. Sea turtles, fish, and seabirds often mistake plastics for food, leading to serious harm.

Factories and agriculture release chemicals that flow into rivers and eventually the sea. These pollutants disturb ecosystems and poison marine animals.

Chemical runoff causes algal blooms, dead zones, and long-term damage to coral reefs and fish populations.
